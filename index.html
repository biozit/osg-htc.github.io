Power your Research with
distributed High Throughput Computing

The OSG Consortium supports researchers with HTC workloads. OSG's fabric of services help researchers and research organizations to support their dHTC compute needs.

Learn how the OSG facilitates dHTC
The OSG is a consortium of research collaborations, campuses, national laboratories and software providers dedicated to the advancement of all open science via the practice of distributed High Throughput Computing (dHTC), and the advancement of its state of the art.

Submit Locally, Run Globally

Access Points provide researchers with a location to place their HTC workloads and data then utilize national-scale resource pools.

Don't have access to a Access Point?

OSG operates an AP, OSGConnect, for the US-based open science community. Using this AP, researchers can acccess the Open Science Pool, a resource pool of opportunistic or donated resources.

OSG makes dHTC impact to all

OSG services allow researchers of all fields of science to ask bigger questions and is not confined to any one field of science. OSG Access Points provide researchers with a home to access compute resources, such as the Open Science Pool, for complex workloads and the Open Science Data Federation for processing large data sets.

New to Large-Scale Computing?

OSG provides a dedicated staff of Research Facilitators here to help you use our services. Research Facilitators can assist with your computational approach, teach you how to place your workloads and data on our Access Points, and connect you with researchers using similar software or methods.

OSG is powered by sharing

The resources accessible through the OSG are contributed by a community of Research Collaborations, Universities and Scientific Institutions. Using OSG provided software and services allows this community to enable the opportunistic usage and sharing of their resources. Over the last 12 months OSG has converted a half billion Core Hours of what would have been idle time into science.

In the NSF's Blueprint for National Cyberinfrastructure Coordination Services the OSG is highlighted for providing access to distributed high throughput computing services to the U.S. research community. To this effect the OSG has provided billions of core hours to researchers all across the U.S., stories of which can be viewed on our User Spotlights page.
